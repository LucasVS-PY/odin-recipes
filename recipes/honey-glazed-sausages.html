Honey Glazed Sausages

Description

It's really no hard work to make sausages from scratch... like the Vikings did. These sausages are packed with good herbs and mustard seeds that pop between your teeth and add a flavoursome kick. Choose a ground pork that is not too lean as it will result in dry sausages.

Ingredients

For about 10 sausages
  • 1-2 garlic cloves, finely chopped
  • 1-2 handfuls fresh herbs like thyme, sage and marjoram, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons thyme, dried
  • 1-2 tablespoons salt
  • 2-3 tablespoons mustard seeds
  • 400 g ground pork
  • 1/2 cup dark beer (100ml)
  • 1-2 pork casings
  • Butter for frying
  • 1-2 tablespoons honey

Steps

  1. Combine the ground pork with garlic, herbs, salt, mustard seeds and beer. Use your hands and make sure it is well mixed.
  2. Stuff the pork into the casings. Make a knot between each sausage.
  3. Place a pot of water over the fire. Add the sausages when the water is lukewarm and slowly let it come to a simmer. Let the sausages cook (simmer) for about 15 minutes.
  4. Grill the sausages on your garden grill or pan fry the sausages in butter and honey. Melt some butter on a hot pan and add 1-2 tablespoons of honey.
  5. Cook the sausages for about 5 minutes on each side until golden and glazed.
  6. Serve the sausages with homemade mustard and a nice flatbread.
